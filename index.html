🎵 How the Sonification Works

Base Frequency (Pitch)

Each planet's pitch is derived from its orbital period using a logarithmic mapping. Faster orbits (shorter periods) produce higher pitches, slower orbits produce lower pitches.

The range is 110 Hz (A2) for Neptune (slowest, ~165 years) to 660 Hz (E5) for Mercury (fastest, ~88 days).

f = 660 × (110/660)ln(T/Tmin) / ln(Tmax/Tmin)

Frequency Scale Slider

The slider applies a multiplier (×0.25 to ×4.0) to all base frequencies, letting you shift the entire sound palette up or down.

♫ Harmonic Mode

Each planet plays a continuous tone built from a unique harmonic profile — a set of overtone amplitudes that shape its wave beyond a simple sine. These are inspired by physical characteristics (mass, rotation speed). A PeriodicWave is built from these harmonics.

A subtle vibrato (frequency modulation via LFO) is applied, with its rate derived from the planet's sidereal rotation period — faster-spinning planets wobble more quickly.

🥁 Beat Mode

Each planet triggers rhythmic percussive hits instead of a continuous tone. The beat rate is derived from the planet's orbital period using a logarithmic mapping:

Mercury (88 d) → 0.18 s  ·  Neptune (60 189 d) → 2.5 s

Faster orbiting planets beat more rapidly, slower ones pulse at a more relaxed pace.

Each hit is composed of three layered sounds:

  • Tonal ping — a short sine burst at the planet's frequency, with a quick pitch drop for a percussive feel.
  • Sub-bass thump — a low-frequency impact weighted by the planet's mass. Gas giants like Jupiter and Saturn produce a heavier thump.
  • Noise transient — a brief bandpass-filtered noise click that adds attack sharpness. The filter Q is also influenced by mass.

All three components follow the same base frequency and frequency scale slider as Harmonic mode.

Volume (Amplitude)

In both modes, volume follows the inverse-square law based on the listener's distance to each planet in the 3D scene:

amplitude = dref² / (dref² + d²)

At distance 0 the volume is 100%; at the reference distance it's 50%, falling off smoothly beyond.
